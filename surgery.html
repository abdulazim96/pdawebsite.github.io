What do I need to know about surgery ?

  • Surgery is a treatment choice for men with earlystage prostate cancer who are in good health.
  • You will be admitted to the hospital for one or more days.
  • The entire prostate gland with the cancer in it will be removed during surgery.
  • Sometimes, the doctor will also remove the lymph glands (nodes) next to the prostate.
  • The surgery usually takes about 2 to 4 hours.
  • The surgery can be performed in several ways :
    • Open prostatectomy
      • The prostate is removed through a single long cut made in the abdomen from a point below your belly button to just above the pubic bone.
    • Laparoscopic / robotic surgery
      • A laparoscope (a long slender tube with a light and camera on the end) is used to remove the prostate.
      • Done through 4 to 6 small cuts in the belly button and the abdomen, instead of a single long cut in the abdomen (key hole surgery).
      • A robot can be used to assist.
      • Less pain, bleeding and require shorter hospital stay.
      • The results of the surgery such as cancer control and long term complications are the same for either open or laparoscopic/robotic surgery.
      • As long as your surgeon is experienced, the type of surgery (open or laparoscopic/robot) usually doesn’t matter.
      • In some cases, the surgeon can do a “nerve-sparing” surgery to lower the chance of complications such as urinary incontinence or erection problem.
      • “nerve-sparing” surgery cannot be done for some men when the nerve is too close to the cancer and the surgeon might have to remove the nerve to lower the chance of leaving the cancer behind.
      • After the surgery
        • A tube (catheter) will be left in your bladder to drain your urine for 1 to 2 weeks to help the wound heal
        • You are advised to rest and avoid strenuous activities for 4-6 weeks before resuming your usual routine activities.
        • You are advised to have a healthy diet and do appropriate physical activities. There is no special food restriction.
      • The cost for open prostatectomy ranges from RM500-2,000 (in government hospitals) to RM20,000-25,000 (in private hospital)

Advantages

  • Offer a cure if the cancer is removed completely.

Disadvantages

  • Sometimes not all cancer cells are removed.
    • Need additional treatment such as radiation to remove remaining cancer cells.
  • Complications of the surgery :
    • Bleeding that require blood transfusion.
    • Blood clots in legs or lungs
    • Wound infection
  • Problems of controlling urine
    • Urine may leak when cough, sneeze, strain or change positions suddenly
    • May last up to several months
    • Don't need special treatment
    • 92 of 100 men get better on their own if they had leaking of urine after surgery. The remaining does not get better
    • Can use pad to protect clothes
    • Or undergo special surgery to control leakage
    • Younger men have fewer problem controlling their urine
  • Problem passing urine
    • Passage of urine improves after surgery for most men
    • Sometimes, scars can be form inside urethra (known as urethral stricture)
      • Occurs in 2-9 men of 100
      • Find hard to pass urine
      • Need procedure to unblock narrowing of urethra
  • Erection Problem Erectile Dysfunction,ED)
    • Problem having an erection
    • Affect feeling about sex and yourself
      • 80 of 100 have long term ED, it depends on extent of the surgery
    • The risk will be lowered if you :
      • Have good erection before surgery
      • Have nerve-sparing surgery
      • Younger
    • Can be treated with :
      • Oral tablets
      • Vacuum devices
      • Injections
      • Penile implant
  • Risk of Death
    • Very rare (may happen in 1-2 men of 1000!)
