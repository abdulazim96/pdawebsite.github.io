What do I need to know about radiotherapy ?

How it works
  • High-energy x-rays are used to destroy cancer cells in the prostate glands.
  • It can be given from either outside (External beam radiotherapy) or inside (brachytherapy) the body.
Cost
  • Government Hospital: RM500 - 5,000
  • Private Hospital: Up to RM15,000

External Beam Radiotherapy (EBRT)

Aim
  • To improve the control of cancer and lower the chance of long term side effects
Procedure
  • Given from outside the body and is directed specifically at the prostate gland.
  • Daily session from Monday to Friday and will take about 10-20 minutes for each sessions.
Pre-treatment
  • Patient has to undergo a planning session called simulation
    • To ensure the radiation is targeted at the area to be treated.
    • To increase the chance of cure.
    • Spare normal tissues as much as possible to reduce side effects.
    • Simulation my involve one or two visits to the hospital
    • Each session will last about 20-30 minutes
Techniques of EBRT
  • Conformal Radiotherapy (CRT)
  • Intensity Modulated Radiation Treatment (IMRT)
  • Image Guided Radiation Treatment (IGRT)
  • Stereotactic Radiotherapy (SRT)

Brachytherapy

Procedure
  • Brachytherapy is directed specifically at the prostate gland but is given from inside the gland itself.
  • It will destroy cancer cells from within the tumour without causing too much damage to the normal tissues around the prostate.
Not suitable for
  • Patient who have undergo surgery to the prostate gland,urinary symptoms or large prostate size.
Pre-treatment
  • Patient has to undergo an ultrasound to guide the insertion of the radioactive seeds or applicator needles.
    • Done in operting room under general anesthesia.
    • Might be able to go home on the same day.

Advantages

Disease is cured
  • If the radiotherapy killed all cancer cells,patient can be free from the disease for the rest of his life.
Lower probability of Erectile Dysfunction
  • Erectile dysfunction is less likely to happen with radiotherapy compared to surgery,but more likely than with observation.
Fewer urine leaking problems
  • Radiotherapy has fewer problems with urine leaking compared to surgery.
Not admitted in hospital
  • Patient do not need to be admitted to the hospital unlike surgery.

Disadvantages

Radiation may not kill all cancer cells.
  • If radiotherapy does not cure your cancer,surgery may be necessary.However surgery may be more difficult because of scarring around the prostate after radiotherapy.
Regular follow up
  • You will be seen regularly by your oncologists during radiotherapy to monitor for and treat side effects that usually will occur two weeks after starting EBRT.
Temporary Side effects
  • Tiredness
  • Mild skin redness and dryness
  • Urinary symptoms
    • Passing urine frequently
    • Urine leakage when feeling of urinate
    • Weak stream
    • Painful urination
    • Blood in the urine
    • Leaking of urine
  • Bowel symptoms
    • Diarrhoea
    • Urgency
    • Painful passing motion
    • Blood in stool
  • Discomfort in abdomen
